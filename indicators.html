Technical Indicators Guide

This guide explains the technical indicators available in the cryptocurrency price chart application and how to interpret them for trading decisions. All indicators work consistently across both supported exchanges (Binance and Kraken).

Exchange Compatibility Note

  • All technical indicators are calculated using the same formulas regardless of the selected exchange
  • Data update frequency may vary between exchanges
  • Some timeframes may not be available on all exchanges
  • Historical data depth might differ between exchanges

Simple Moving Average (SMA-20)

What It Is

The Simple Moving Average (SMA) calculates the average price over a specified period (20 periods in our implementation). It helps identify the overall trend direction and potential support/resistance levels. The SMA line smooths out price fluctuations to show the general trend.

How to Read It

  • When price is above SMA: Generally bullish trend - indicates upward momentum
  • When price is below SMA: Generally bearish trend - indicates downward momentum
  • Price crossing SMA: Potential trend reversal - signals a change in momentum
  • SMA slope: Indicates trend strength and direction - steeper slope means stronger trend

Trading Signals

Trend Following

  • Enter long when price crosses above SMA (bullish crossover)
  • Exit long when price crosses below SMA (bearish crossover)
  • The steeper the SMA slope, the stronger the trend momentum
  • Flat SMA suggests consolidation or ranging market

Support/Resistance

  • SMA often acts as dynamic support in uptrends - price bounces off SMA
  • SMA often acts as dynamic resistance in downtrends - price rebounds down from SMA
  • Multiple tests of the SMA can indicate strong support/resistance levels
  • Price breaking through SMA with high volume suggests stronger trend change

Relative Strength Index (RSI-14)

What It Is

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate overbought or oversold conditions. It oscillates between 0 and 100, with traditional overbought/oversold levels at 70 and 30.

How to Read It

  • RSI > 70: Potentially overbought conditions - market may be due for a pullback
  • RSI < 30: Potentially oversold conditions - market may be due for a bounce
  • RSI = 50: Neutral level - often used as a trend indicator
  • Divergences between RSI and price can signal potential reversals

Trading Signals

Overbought/Oversold

  • Consider selling when RSI moves above 70 - market is overextended to upside
  • Consider buying when RSI moves below 30 - market is overextended to downside
  • More extreme readings (>80 or <20) indicate stronger signals
  • Wait for RSI to start reversing before taking action

Divergences

  • Bullish divergence: Price makes lower lows while RSI makes higher lows - potential reversal up
  • Bearish divergence: Price makes higher highs while RSI makes lower highs - potential reversal down
  • Divergences are stronger when they occur in overbought/oversold territory

Trend Confirmation

  • RSI staying above 50 in uptrends - indicates bullish momentum
  • RSI staying below 50 in downtrends - indicates bearish momentum
  • Crossing 50 can signal trend changes - watch for confirmation

Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)

What It Is

MACD is a trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of an asset's price. It consists of the MACD line (difference between 12 and 26-period EMAs), Signal line (9-period EMA of MACD line), and Histogram (difference between MACD and Signal lines).

How to Read It

  • MACD above zero: Bullish momentum - faster MA above slower MA
  • MACD below zero: Bearish momentum - faster MA below slower MA
  • Histogram shows momentum strength and potential reversals
  • Signal line crossovers indicate potential entry/exit points

Trading Signals

Signal Line Crossovers

  • Bullish: MACD crosses above signal line - potential buy signal
  • Bearish: MACD crosses below signal line - potential sell signal
  • Stronger signals when occurring above/below zero line
  • Look for crossovers with increasing histogram height for confirmation

Zero Line Crossovers

  • Bullish: MACD crosses above zero - indicates trend change to bullish
  • Bearish: MACD crosses below zero - indicates trend change to bearish
  • Major trend change signals - often more reliable than signal line crosses

Divergences

  • Bullish divergence: Price makes lower lows while MACD makes higher lows
  • Bearish divergence: Price makes higher highs while MACD makes lower highs
  • Divergences suggest potential trend exhaustion and reversal

Using Indicators Together

Automated Recommendations

The application's recommendation system combines multiple technical analysis approaches to identify potential short-term trading opportunities. Here's how it works:

Analysis Components

  • Price Momentum (40% weight)
    • Analyzes 30 one-minute candles
    • Calculates recent price change percentage
    • Must show positive momentum
  • Volume Analysis (40% weight)
    • Compares current volume to 30-minute average
    • Requires minimum 20% volume increase
    • Higher volume increases likelihood of price movement
  • Volatility Assessment (20% weight)
    • Measures average price movement per minute
    • Ensures sufficient volatility for 1% movement
    • Helps confirm trading opportunity viability

Selection Process

  1. Filters top 100 coins by trading volume
  2. Calculates composite score using weighted metrics
  3. Requires minimum score of 0.5 for consideration
  4. Selects top 5 opportunities with highest scores

Trading Strategy

  • Designed for short-term trades (15-30 minutes)
  • Target profit: 1% gain from entry price
  • Best used during active market hours
  • Consider setting stop-loss below recent support levels

Important Considerations

  • Recommendations are based on technical analysis only
  • Always check fundamental factors before trading
  • Market conditions can change rapidly
  • Use proper position sizing and risk management
  • Monitor trades actively when using short-term strategies

Trend Confirmation

  1. Use SMA for primary trend direction
  2. Confirm with RSI staying above/below 50
  3. Look for MACD zero line crossovers in the same direction

Entry Points

  1. Wait for price to cross SMA in trend direction
  2. Check RSI isn't in extreme territory
  3. Look for MACD signal line crossover

Exit Points

  1. Price crossing SMA against trend
  2. RSI reaching overbought/oversold levels
  3. MACD divergence or signal line crossover

Risk Management

  • Don't rely on any single indicator
  • Look for confluence between multiple indicators
  • Consider broader market conditions
  • Use appropriate stop losses
  • Monitor trading volume for confirmation

Important Notes

No Perfect Indicator

  • All indicators can give false signals
  • Use multiple timeframes for confirmation
  • Consider fundamental analysis alongside technical indicators

Market Conditions

  • Indicators work best in trending markets
  • Different indicators suit different market conditions
  • Adjust strategies based on market volatility

Best Practices

  • Start with one or two indicators
  • Add more as you understand each one
  • Practice in different market conditions
  • Keep detailed trading notes
  • Always use proper risk management
